Key Milestones

2025

Empowered over 100 Rohingya refugee children through 34 weekend literacy and numeracy sessions, expanded with support from Open Feed NGO and AIU faculty.

2024

Delivered essential food supplies to flood-affected families and Rohingya refugee children in a madrasah where AMSA volunteers regularly teach.

2021

2020

Raised RM15,000 for COVID-19 relief efforts through various fundraising campaigns.

What People Say About Us

Pyae Sone Kyaw
Sharifah Rokiah Award Receipient, AIU 2025 Convocation

"My journey with AMSA shaped some of the most meaningful years of my life. Serving as the Male Treasurer for two consecutive terms, as well as contributing to the Religious Unit, Arts & Culture Unit, and volunteering as a HOPE Project teacher, allowed me to grow both personally and professionally. The experiences, challenges, and community work I was entrusted with taught me responsibility, empathy, and leadership far beyond the classroom. Receiving the Vice Chancellor’s Award and the Sharifah Rokiah Award during my graduation was a humbling reminder of how impactful AMSA has been in my development. I am truly grateful to AMSA for the opportunities, guidance, and friendships that continue to inspire me today."
